How age works

One adventure, four experiences

The assessment estimates a developmental age (not just birthday age) and sets two dials: a band (which experience leads) and a difficulty tier (how every game tunes itself — sizes, speeds, numbers, reading).

🌱 Band 1 · Little Sprout
≈ 6–18 months

Screens teach little at this age — the Parent & Baby Quest Library leads (2 real-world quests/day, ~5 min). Screen games hidden.

🐾 Band 2 · Little Explorer
≈ 18 mo – 2½ yrs

Daily parent quests still lead; games open as dessert on the tiny tier — biggest targets, slowest speeds, numbers 1–3, zero reading.

🎒 Band 3 · Little Adventurer
≈ 2½ – 4 yrs

Games step up to the small tier — numbers to ~5, picture hints stay on, still no reading required.

🚀 School Age
≈ 5 – 12 yrs

The world map leads on the big tier — reading decks unlock, numbers to ~20, speeds climb, and the Stroop-style challenges appear.

Firefly Forest · Whispering Signposts · Echo Caves — first-generation titles

Maze Runner fine motor — trace 5 mazes without touching walls (precision, smoothness, lift-offs). Word Wizard reading — match words to pictures. Sense Explorer sensory — match colors, sounds & patterns.

Shared across every game

✨ Starlight, levels & companions

✨ Starlight

Earned for finishing any quest — effort floor means wrong answers reduce but never zero the payout. Never shown as a grade.

🎖️ Levels

Each game remembers its highest level beaten and offers the next (faster, smaller, longer, more crowded). Explorer level grows with total starlight.

🐢🦊🦉🦄🐉 Companions

Shelly ✨30 · Flick ✨80 · Sage ✨150 · Nova ✨250 · Ember ✨400 — sleeping silhouettes until unlocked; the hook for future real-world rewards.

Every game logs only: child profile id, domain, task, difficulty, accuracy, attempts, timing — consent-gated, on-device, parent-deletable (see SECURITY.md & Privacy Policy).
